about this list

This is a curated collection of Roblox games within the transfur/infection genre — a niche but passionate corner of the platform. These games range from abandoned experiments to active communities, from rough prototypes to polished experiences.

why this list exists

The transfur/infection genre has a scattered history. Many games come and go, get deleted, get abandoned, or get lost in the noise. This page exists to document what's out there. I personally made this list to document these games because people back in 2020–2022 during the pandemic were already trying to cancel Transfur games left and right and they're still doing it, just at a smaller scale. Their primary argument is "It's a game related to a fetish game." But it wasn't. At least not literally or directly. Most of these games didn't deserve that shit treatment. This genre was something new and never-seen before. It was nothing more than a genre where you kill Furries. (Heavy emphasis on killing Furries.) That genre was a chance for players to escape boredom and finally play something fresh on this stale platform called ROBLOX. Any references to "Changed" were purely artistic. Tiny fucking references, so specific you wouldn't randomly go "Oh, I totally fucking know that" unless you have an asshole-shaped radar for micro-references and unrelated connections. And all of these words came from a "Furry hater", think about it. Hotdog buns aren't baguettes, even though they share the same ingredient.

how this website works

Data is pulled from games-data.json, which is updated automatically every 6 hours via GitHub Actions. Player counts, thumbnails, and game details are fetched directly from Roblox APIs. Tags and maintainer notes are added manually based on community knowledge and personal testing.

the criteria

A game is included if:

  • It's within the transfur / infection / furry infection genre
  • Isnt a clear spit in the face to the Transfur Genre
  • It's a remake, reupload, or spiritual successor to a classic
  • It's simply a hidden gem worth documenting

Games are not included based on quality alone — dead games, broken games, and obscure experiments are all welcome. This is an archive, not a recommendation engine. (Although this website wasnt made for pure archival)

tags explained

  • unknown-quality — haven't played to judge
  • low-quality — really rough around the edges
  • moderate-quality — decent, playable, some effort
  • high-quality — polished, well-made, recommended
  • supreme-quality — exceptional, genre-defining
  • OG — original or early classic in the genre
