about this list

This is a collection of Roblox games within the transfur/infection genre - a niche but passionate corner of the platform. These games range from abandoned experiments to active communities, from rough prototypes to polished experiences.

why this list exists

 The transfur/infection genre has a scattered history. Many games come and go, get deleted, get abandoned, or get lost in the noise.

I made this list because during the pandemic people tried to cancel Transfur games left and right, and the stigma stayed. Yes, Changed was the inspiration. Are the Roblox versions "fetish" games? No. Calling them that ignores what they actually are.

Most of these games didn't deserve that treatment. This genre was something new, a game where you kill furries. That's it. (Heavy emphasis on kill.)

That genre gave players something fresh on a stale platform. Any references to Changed were tiny details, so small you wouldn't notice them unless you were looking for trouble, and knew what to look for.

how this website works

Data is pulled from games-data.json, which is updated automatically every 6 hours via GitHub Actions. Player counts, thumbnails, and game details are fetched directly from Roblox APIs. Tags and maintainer notes are added manually based on community knowledge and personal testing.

the criteria

A game is included if:

  • It's within the transfur / infection / furry infection genre
  • Isn't a clear spit in the face to the Transfur Genre
  • It's a remake, reupload, or spiritual successor to a classic
  • It's simply a hidden gem worth documenting

Games are not included based on quality alone - dead games, broken games, and obscure experiments are all welcome. This is an archive, not a recommendation engine. (Although this website wasnt made for pure archival)

tags explained

  • unknown-quality - haven't played to judge
  • low-quality - visible low or zero effort
  • moderate-quality - decent, playable, some effort
  • high-quality - polished, well-made, recommended
  • supreme-quality - exceptional, self-explainatory
  • OG - original or early classic in the genre (or is OG-like)
