about this list

This is a curated collection of Roblox games within the transfur/infection genre — a niche but passionate corner of the platform. These games range from abandoned experiments to active communities, from rough prototypes to polished experiences.

why this list exists

The transfur/infection genre has a scattered history. Many games come and go, get deleted, get abandoned, or get lost in the noise.

I made this list because during the pandemic people tried to cancel Transfur games left and right, and the stigma stayed. Yes, Changed was the inspiration. Are the Roblox versions "fetish" games? No. Calling them that ignores what they actually are.

Most of these games didn't deserve that treatment. This genre was something new, a game where you kill furries. That's it. Heavy emphasis on kill.

That genre gave players something fresh on a stale platform. Any references to Changed were tiny details, so small you wouldn't notice them unless you were looking for trouble, and knew what to look for.

These words came from someone who doesn't like furries. Think about that.

Hotdog buns aren't baguettes, even though they share the same ingredient.
(lets be real)

Swap the furries with zombies and suddenly the "controversy" disappears. That's the whole point. There's no hidden agenda. No fetish content. Just a game where you fight monsters.

I'm a furry hater. I don't like furries. But I like these games because they're games where you kill furries. That's it. I'm not reframing. I'm not playing politics. I'm telling you what these games actually are from a Roblox player's perspective.

Yes, these games exist because of Changed. No, they aren't fetish games. No adult content. Nothing to flag. And to most of the playerbase, that's all there is to it.

If that makes you uncomfortable, ask yourself why. If swapping furries with zombies makes the controvery dissapear then the problem was never the game. It was the association.

Hotdog buns aren't baguettes, even though they share the same ingredient. An origin isn't an identity. A starting point isn't a destination. The game is what's in front of you, not what inspired it.

the criteria

A game is included if:

  • It's within the transfur / infection / furry infection genre
  • Isn't a clear spit in the face to the Transfur Genre
  • It's a remake, reupload, or spiritual successor to a classic
  • It's simply a hidden gem worth documenting

Games are not included based on quality alone — dead games, broken games, and obscure experiments are all welcome. This is an archive, not a recommendation engine. (Although this website wasnt made for pure archival)

tags explained

  • unknown-quality — haven't played to judge
  • low-quality — really rough around the edges
  • moderate-quality — decent, playable, some effort
  • high-quality — polished, well-made, recommended
  • supreme-quality — exceptional, genre-defining
  • OG — original or early classic in the genre
